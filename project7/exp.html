Experience


Since the start of my college career, I have had an interest in the intersection between Linguistics and Technology. With my combined majors, I offer a unique perspective on the affordances one has when interacting with an information system, especially concerning the language that is used within these systems. Through my experiences as a student, a mentor, and a research assistant, I have been able to gain an understanding of how important inclusivity and accessibility in the realm of human computer interaction is.

Presentations

I presented research findings at various research festivals and presentations. Read more below.

In Spring 2024, I took LIN406 Field Methods with Dr. Adam Singerman. Our class worked with a native speaker consultant of Ancash Quechua (AQ) to build a general understanding of the broad characteristics of the language and its verbal morphology. At the end of the semester, I presented my findings with my classmates at the Linguistic Studies Research Fest hosted by the Langauges, Literature, and Linguistics department at Syracuse University.

From May 2023-May 2024, I was a Research Assistant with the Syracuse Office of Undergraduate Research and Creative Engagement (SOURCE). Led by Professors Mark Linder and Emily Pellicano from the School of Architecture, I collaborated with two other students in a transdisciplinary research project tracking the reporting and usage of Artificial Intelligence. We presented our preliminary findings at the Fall 2023 SOURCE Resesarch Expo.

In Spring 2024, I took LIN431 Phonological Analysis with Dr. Chris Green, in which we analyzed the Nupoid languages. Our class was given raw data collected by Professor Constanze Weise (Eastern Tennessee State University) and her team in Nigeria to analyze for phonological rules and characteristics. I worked with a graduate student on Kami, specifically, and later presented the class' general findings at the Spring 2024 College of Arts and Sciences Research Fair with my classmates.
